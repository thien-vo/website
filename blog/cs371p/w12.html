CS371p Spring 2017: Thien Vo

Weekly blog for Object-Oriented Programming

Week 12: 04/10/2017 - 04/16/2017

What did you do this past week?

This past week, I was quite productive overall. I finished all of my competitive programming homework on Monday, and I started to work on my last OOP project with my partner, Calvin Liu, on Wednesday. By Friday, we got the algorithm and logic mostly working. We haven't had time to work on it over the weekend, but I'm sure we'll finish just fine. Also, I finished brainstorming for an essay for my culture class, so I can just start writing it tomorrow.

What's in your way?

Probability class has been kicking my ass. I'm a little bit behind, and I need to catch up on soon, or I will be in big trouble. Also, registration is coming up and I still have not made some concrete plans yet, so I need to do that soon.

What will you do next week?

First, I'll plan my schedule out thoroughly so I can register for classes on Friday. I have plan to finish my essay by Tuesday to turn it in on Wednesday's night the latest. Also, one of my friend's birthday is coming on this Thursday, so I will want to make something happen for that :)

What's my experience of the class?

Professor Downing spent 2 days talking about the projects. It was quite helpful, because it gave us a lot of insight on the project and possible problems that we may encounter. However, I think that Professor Downing could have gone over inheritance first, because my partner and I started early, so we figured out most of the inheritance stuff online, then we had to listen to it again in class.

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the week?

My pick-of-the-week is a book called "Solve for Happy: Engineer Your Path to Joy" by Mo Gawdat. Mo was inspired to write a book in hope of helping millions of other people finding their happinesses. This heart-breaking background gives the readers further insight into this astounding, well-written book.
