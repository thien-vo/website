CS371p Spring 2017: Thien Vo

Weekly blog for Object-Orieted Programming

Week 7: 02/27/2017 - 03/14/2017

What did you do this past week?

This past week, I got a lot of my assignments and midterms done. I had a probability exam on Tuesday, and I think I did fairly well. I had a paper due for my culture class on Wednesday, and I turned it in the night before. I spent about 6 hours studying for OOp exam. Out of those 6 hours, 2 of them were for making a cheat sheet. I notice that a lot of people use this same cheat sheet that someone posted on PIazza. However, I only used it as a reference to compare with mine. I feel like making the cheat sheet helped me review the material a lot. The rest of the 6 hours was a mix of studying by myself by going over readings and quizzes, and studying with a partner just doing a lot of coding samples to make sure we understand the materials. I did really well on the exam.

What's in your way?

I have an exam in my culture class this week, and 2 assignments due for probability. I think we are also getting project 3 soon for OOP, so I'm looking forward to that.

What will you do next week?

I have to finish my probability homeworks, so I am going to spend most of the time doing that. Other than that, my coworker and I decided to pair up and work on some projects together. We are going to develop a small android game as our first exposure to mobile app development. Hence, this week, we are going to get comfortable with the IDE and API.

What's my experience of the class?

The class does not provide any review for the exam. For me, the exam wasn't particularly difficult, but I was expecting some sort of overview of the material. Professor spent the last day before the exam teaching us some material that could be on the exam, and even though it wasn't on exam, I made me feel rushed and unprepared. Other than that, everything else is fine.

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the week?

My pick-of-the-week Coursera. I'm surprised that a lot of people do not know a lot of online courses platform like Coursera, Udemy, EdX. These platforms provide opportunities for you to obtain certifications, or audit courses for free. This is a great way for you to get into something you like without having to wait to take the class in person at the university.
