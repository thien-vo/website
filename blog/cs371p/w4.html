What did you do this past week?

This past week, I started on Voting Australia project. My partner and I were able to read in the input to our desired data structure. We are not finished solving it yet, but it should be straight forward with this data structure. Also, I participated in a competitive programming contest this Friday. It was incredibly fun. Next week, I'm going finish Voting Australia strong and submit it preferably Wednesday night. I really need to study for my government exam on Thursday. What's my experience of the class?

OOP is the only class that I haven't been distracted during the lecture. Thanks to Prof's no eletronic policy, the class is very engaging and moves very quickly. One of the quiz for this week was a little bit glitchy, I think. I never ran out of time, but I ran out of time before the last question for this quiz. A lot of other people in the class complained, so I'm glad I was not the only one. Oh well, I know Prof Downing drops numerous quizzes at the end, and we also have some free ones.

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the week?

My pick-of-the-week is Haskell. Haskell is a great functional programming language. I have to learn Haskell for one of my class, and I like it so far. The way it makes you think about a problem logically gives me a better thinking mentality as a programmer. You can go to learnyouahaskell.com to learn more.
