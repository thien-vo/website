CS371p Spring 2017: Thien Vo

Weekly blog for Object-Orieted Programming

Week 5: 02/20/2017 - 02/26/2017

What did you do this past week?

This past week, I had an interview with JP Morgan. I think it went pretty well. The only thing that bothers me is the technical interview I had with their engineer. Well... It wasn't very technical in my opinions. He only asked me several questions about my projects and my skillset. I was very surprised that I didn't have to answer any coding problems. Glassdoor interview reviews said that the technical interviews were very intense for them, so that's why it bothers me. Anyway, it's done, and I hope to hear back from them this coming week. Also, I caught up on with probability class this past week. I am going to have an exam this Tuesday, so that's good news for me.

What's in your way?

Program languages. I'm considering Q-drop that class because I just simply do not have any motivation to attend the class even. I just get very confused throughout the class, and a lot of my friends say the same thing. Also, I just don't know how this class will be anything useful for my career.

What will you do next week?

I have two exams this coming week, probability and OOP. I'm more prepared for probability, but for OOP, I am worried about it. I heard my friends, who had taken the class in the past semesters, said that the exams are rather challenging. I will need to spend some good time to study for this exam.

What's my experience of the class?

The quizzes are getting on my nerves. I usually do my quizzes on my phone. Several times, they give me a popup warning saying that I have 10 seconds left. Then, when I click "X" to close the popup so I can choose an answer and submit, it just submit the quiz without letting pick my answer even though I still have 8 seconds left. So, if you can take quizzes on laptop, I would highly recommend so.

What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the week?

My pick-of-the-week is ACM, a CS organization. I recently joined them this semester, and I've been making a lot of genuine connections there. It is pretty much a social club. We usually have programming contests every other week. We also always have some form of event every Friday. It could be a gaming night, a board game night, or a family dinner night. You get to meet a lot of people in CS, and some of them might even be in your classes!
