About Me

A few words about me

I came to the United States of America in March, 2010. I spent my first year in a small town called Levittown in Pennsylvania. With 98% of the population comprised of non-Asian, the culture shock in addition to the language barrier all pointed me to failure. At school, I was placed in Pre-Algebra at 7th grade (at this time, I have already studied Pre-Calculus in Vietnam) because the school administrators believe that English would prevent me to do math. In 8th grade, my Algebra 1 teacher, Mrs. Diamond, told me that I should be in at least Geometry class right now, if not Algebra 2.

I realized that I cannot count on the system to help me achieve the American Dream.

I was stuck in the English as Second Langauge (ESL) system. I couldn't get out even if I wanted to. My ESL teacher told me that I cannot graduate the system until I'm in 10th grade or so. This system prevented me from taking Honors classes that would help me build a good GPA for college. When my parents told me that we were moving to Texas during the summer of 2011, I knew that this was my only chance. So, I spent the last 3 or 4 months in Pennsylvania honing my English so that during my next placement exam for the same system in Texas. I promised that I would get myself out of this damn system, and I did. Then, I was able to take on-level classes just like everyone else who was born here. I moved quickly up the ladder though. I enrolled in my first Honors English class in 10th grade, then AP English Composition and AP English Literature come afterward. I expanded my friend circle to meet with other people from different cultures: Mexico, India, Nigeria, England, and so many more!

When I get accepted to UT Austin with a scholarship, I knew that I am one step closer to the American Dream .

Now that I am a Senior majoring in Computer Science, I plan to finish my degree strong. I am eagerly looking forward to contributing my diligence and intelligence to the tech industry that is shaping the entire world day by day.
